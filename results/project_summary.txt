================================================================================
PROJECT SUMMARY: NJ CLIMATE RISK, CHAMP COHORTS, AND MUNICIPAL BOND TRADES
================================================================================

1. CURRENT STATUS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This repository now supports an exploratory screening analysis, not a final causal
estimate of NJDEP/CHAMP effects on municipal borrowing costs. The available bond
data are secondary-market WRDS trades, and spreads are calculated against a
deterministic synthetic AAA benchmark rather than a validated Bloomberg/MMD AAA
curve. The results should therefore be framed as diagnostic associations.

2. DATA PIPELINE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The current regression panel combines:
- WRDS secondary-market New Jersey municipal GO-like trades.
- A synthetic AAA benchmark used to calculate spread_bps.
- Municipality-level 4-ft SLR tax-base exposure from
  data/data_cleaned/climate/nj_statewide_frm_scores_FINAL.csv.
- Available CHAMP applicant rows parsed from local CHAMP PDFs and mapped to
  canonical municipalities using raw applicant names.
- UFB debt-to-assessed-value controls and ACS income controls.

The panel now preserves time_to_maturity, ever_champ, first_champ_sfy,
best_champ_rank, crs_resilient, and a time-varying is_resilient flag that turns
on at the start of the first observed CHAMP state fiscal year.

3. UPDATED ECONOMETRIC RESULT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The primary diagnostic specification uses:
- towns with both pre- and post-2023 CHAMP-period trade observations;
- 1st/99th percentile winsorized spreads to reduce the influence of extreme
  secondary-trade outliers;
- municipality and year fixed effects;
- time-to-maturity controls in the fixed-effect DiD and triple-difference
  models; debt-to-AV is excluded from those two specifications;
- clustered standard errors by municipality.

The updated triple-difference coefficient for:
    ever_champ x post_2023 x slr_exposure_pct
is +0.105 bps per 1 percentage point of SLR exposure, with p=0.652.

This is not statistically distinguishable from zero. The prior headline claim
that proactive resilience is actively penalized by the bond market is not
supported by the corrected panel and model.

4. PRE-TREND AND IDENTIFICATION LIMITS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The pre-treatment trend check is not clean. The CHAMP cohort has a statistically
different pre-2023 trend in winsorized spreads:
    beta = -4.036 bps/year, p=0.0033.

That weakens a causal DiD interpretation. A policy-facing report should not claim
that the 2023 CHAMP-period cutoff caused spread changes without a stronger
design, validated treatment timing, and better bond-level controls.

5. REMAINING LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Synthetic AAA is not a substitute for same-date, same-maturity Bloomberg/MMD AAA.
- Secondary-market trades are not equivalent to issuance/offering yields.
- Rating, insurance, tax status, callability, issue size, trade size, and CUSIP
  liquidity controls are still missing from the available regression panel.
- CHAMP treatment is based only on locally parsed applicant PDFs. Additional IUP,
  Water Bank, and CHAMP files may change treatment timing and cohort membership.
- The panel covers 195 municipalities after matching and control requirements,
  not all New Jersey municipalities.
- 2020 is absent from the bond panel.

6. POLICY INTERPRETATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The defensible current conclusion is:

In the available exploratory secondary-trade panel, there is no robust statistical
evidence that high-SLR-exposure CHAMP-cohort municipalities were penalized after
the 2023 CHAMP-period cutoff. Further work is needed before drawing conclusions
about market pricing of NJDEP resilience programs or municipal borrowing cost
impacts.
